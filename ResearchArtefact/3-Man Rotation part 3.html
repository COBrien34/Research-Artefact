Rocket League- A deep dive into 3-man rotation- part 3

This is the final rotation, this is when it comes full circle. Lets look at the state of play, for the orange team there is a player ready to pass the ball center to the player waiting in midfield, and there is a player anchoring the defense. For the blue team there is a player in goal and is the last line of defense, there is a player rotating on the weak side of the field, and the other player is out of the play and also looking to rotate back.

In this situation the player in goal for blue team goes for the ball and it gets stuck in between both players going for the ball, and is sitting near the goal, the player rotating on the back side of the field can now rotate in and clean up the play, the initial player made. The rotating player does not need to worry about the midfield player for the orange team because they are too far away to commit to the ball.

And that completes a full overall rotation for the team. The reason why the 3-man rotation is implemented into how a team plays, is that it creates a fluid structure to the game, rather than just everyone on the pitch bumping into players on their own team. Having a structured way of playing makes sure that you can manipulate the game in the way you want, and take full control of the game.
